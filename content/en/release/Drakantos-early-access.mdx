<Section icon={Swords} title="New Combat and Movement Mechanics">
One of the most significant updates in the Drakantos early access beta was the overhaul of the movement and combat systems. The original tile-based grid system, which sometimes felt laggy and restrictive, has been replaced with a more fluid, freeform movement. This change aims to provide a more seamless and intuitive combat experience for players.

### Movement and Combat Evolution

| Feature           | Previous Beta (Tile-Based) | Second Beta (Freeform)      | Impact on Gameplay                                   |
|:------------------|:---------------------------|:----------------------------|:-----------------------------------------------------|
| **Movement**      | Grid-based, square tiles   | 360-degree freeform         | More fluid, less restrictive movement.               |
| **Combat System** | Hybrid, target-based       | Full action combat, no target system | Direct aiming, more dynamic skill usage.             |
| **Mouse Controls**| Indirect targeting         | Direct aim with mouse       | Enhanced precision for attacks and skills.           |
| **Controller Support**| Less optimized           | Smoother, 360-degree movement | Improved fluidity for controller players.            |
| **Basic Attacks** | Cooldown dependent         | Stamina-like system, outside cooldown | Allows for continuous action, strategic stamina management. |

<Callout type="warning" title="Adjusting to New Controls">
For players accustomed to the previous tile-based system, the new freeform movement and action combat will require some adjustment. Heroes might feel different to play, so dedicate time to re-learn and experiment with the updated controls and combat flow.
</Callout>

<Section icon={Trophy} title="Exploring New PvP Modes">
The second Drakantos early access beta heavily emphasized PvP, introducing several distinct game modes to test player skill and strategy. These modes offer varied objectives and experiences for competitive players.

### PvP Modes Overview

| Mode      | Objective                                         | Rewards                 | Strategy                                           |
|:----------|:--------------------------------------------------|:------------------------|:---------------------------------------------------|
| **Control** | Maintain control over a designated circular zone. | Moderate, faster achievements | Area denial, sustained presence, team coordination. |

</Callout>

### Considerations for Early Access
Player feedback during early access and beta tests is invaluable. Issues like unintuitive UI, combat latency, and unfair monster scaling, as highlighted by some beta testers, are crucial points for the developers to address. The potential for Drakantos to be a hit is high, especially with its beautiful pixel art and nostalgic MMO vibe, but refinement is key.

| Aspect         | Beta Feedback                                      | Developer Goal (Implied)                               |
|:---------------|:---------------------------------------------------|:-------------------------------------------------------|
| **UI**         | Basic, unintuitive, detracts from visuals.         | Enhance visual appeal and user-friendliness.           |
| **Combat Latency**| Slow, unresponsive, especially for spellcasting. | Reduce latency, improve responsiveness for smoother combat. |
| **Monster Difficulty**| Inconsistent (too easy or Dark Souls-level). | Balance difficulty curve for consistent challenge.      |
| **Party Scaling**| Missions easier solo than with a party.            | Adjust monster scaling for cooperative play.           |
| **Mana Management**| Limited, encourages cooldown-spamming.           | Introduce more strategic resource management.           |
| **Artifact Uses**| Limited uses per mission.                          | Re-evaluate artifact mechanics for better utility.       |
| **AI Voices**  | Jarring, detracts from immersion.                  | Improve voice acting quality or explore alternatives.    |
