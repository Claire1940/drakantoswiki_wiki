<Section icon={Swords} title="Gameplay Mechanics and Combat Style">

Drakantos distinguishes itself from modern action MMORPGs by embracing a more deliberate, old-school combat pace. The game features a stationary attack system where characters are locked into animations during their abilities. This design choice emphasizes strategy and positioning over mindless button mashing, making every "big bonk" attack feel impactful and significant.

- **Hero System**: Instead of generic classes, players choose from specific heroes with deep backstories and unique mechanical traits.
- **Movement**: The game utilizes a classic sprite-based system. While you can move diagonally, the character sprites remain fixed in four directions to preserve the retro aesthetic.
- **Skill Customization**: Abilities can be modified using Orbs and Artifacts. For example, some orbs might add energy regeneration or damage reduction to your core skills.
- **PvP Environment**: Competitive modes ensure even stats, meaning only skill and strategy determine the victor in ranked matches and tournaments.

<Callout type="success" title="Developer Insight">
The game director has confirmed that the lack of diagonal sprites is an intentional artistic choice to maintain the "old school" legitimacy of the pixel art style.
</Callout>

While the combat may feel slow initially, it is perfectly tuned for the game's challenging PvE content. Bosses like the Griffin require players to learn telegraphs and time their dodges or parries perfectly. This high-stakes environment makes group play in the open world or dungeons highly rewarding, as a single mistake can lead to a full team wipe.

</Section>
