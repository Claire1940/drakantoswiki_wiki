<Section icon={Swords} title="Dungeon Crawling & Combat Mechanics">
The core of Drakantos' progression lies within its three-man dungeons. These instanced missions are your primary source of experience and valuable loot. You can queue for dungeons from anywhere in the world, and missions refresh every 20-30 minutes, offering a variety of objectives such as escort, extermination, or item retrieval. To maximize your rewards, it's always best to clear all mobs within a dungeon.

<Callout type="warning" title="Death Penalty">
Dying during a dungeon mission will result in an experience loss, approximately 5% of your total experience. Plan your engagements carefully!
</Callout>

Combat in Drakantos is described as tactical, requiring precise timing of your skills and cooldowns. While movement can feel a bit stiff or grid-based initially, players quickly adapt. Each hero gains skills as they level up, becoming fully defined around level 12 when their core defensive or healing abilities unlock.

| Dungeon Mission Type | Objective | Key Strategy | Rewards |
| :------------------- | :-------- | :----------- | :------ |
| **Exterminate**      | Defeat all enemies | Group mobs, use AoE | XP, Gear, Materials |
| **Escort**           | Protect NPC to destination | Clear path, defend target | XP, Gear, Materials |
| **Item Hunt**        | Find scattered items | Explore map thoroughly | XP, Gear, Materials |

<Callout type="info" title="Skill Unlocking">
Most classes define their core role and gain their primary defensive or healing skill around level 12. This significantly impacts gameplay.
</Callout>
</Section>

<Section icon={Trophy} title="Progression Systems: Gear, Orbs & Artifacts">
Progression in Drakantos is heavily tied to your gear score and the strategic use of orbs and artifacts. Gear consists of a hat, top, shoes, and a weapon, alongside two artifact slots. Artifacts are powerful items that grant unique skills, allowing for significant customization of your playstyle with effects like damage over time, healing, or crowd control.

<Steps>
  <Step num="1" title="Prioritize Orbs">
    Collect orbs from dungeons. These orbs are used to enhance specific skills, providing bonuses like increased damage or defensive capabilities. Once used, an orb is bound to that skill and character.
  </Step>
  <Step num="2" title="Acquire & Upgrade Gear">
    Focus on getting the highest tier of gear available from dungeons. Use materials gathered from the open world to reinforce and boost the gear score of your items at the blacksmith.
  </Step>
  <Step num="3" title="Customize with Artifacts">
    Equip two artifacts to modify your playstyle. Experiment with different combinations (e.g., damage, healing, crowd control) to find what suits your hero and team composition best. Artifacts refresh their skills after each dungeon run.
  </Step>
  <Step num="4" title="Advance Dungeon Tiers">
    As your gear score and character strengthen, tackle higher-tier dungeons. These offer greater challenges but yield superior experience and loot, propelling your progression further.
  </Step>
</Steps>

Your gear score is paramount; it dictates roughly 99% of your character's effectiveness. A higher gear score directly translates to more health for tanks, and presumably more damage for DPS, making it the most critical factor for advancing to more difficult content.

| Progression Element | Purpose | Acquisition Method | Impact |
| :------------------ | :------ | :----------------- | :----- |
| **Gear**            | Base stats, higher tiers | Dungeon drops | **Massive Gear Score boost** |
| **Orbs**            | Skill enhancement | Dungeon drops | Significant skill power increase |
| **Artifacts**       | Unique active abilities | Dungeon drops, quests | Playstyle customization |
| **Materials**       | Gear reinforcement | Open world gathering | Increases Gear Score of items |

<Callout type="success" title="Smart Inventory Management">
While equipped gear cannot be transferred, higher-level gear found in dungeons can be stored in your inventory and used by other characters, aiding in alt progression.
</Callout>
</Section>

<Section icon={Shield} title="Open World Activities & Resource Management">
The open world in Drakantos plays a supportive role to your dungeon-focused progression. It's the primary location for questing, which provides lore and minor rewards, and more importantly, for gathering essential materials. These materials are crucial for reinforcing your dungeon-acquired gear, directly contributing to your gear score.

<Callout type="info" title="Adventurer's Guild">
Visit the Adventurer's Guild in the inn to spend coins earned from missions. You can buy tickets to boost material gains, experience, or loot, and even acquire vanity items.
</Callout>

You'll find various resource nodes like trees and mining spots, along with lockpickable chests that can yield materials and tickets. While the open world doesn't offer significant experience from combat, it's vital for sustaining your gear upgrades.
