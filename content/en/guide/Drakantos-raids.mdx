### Current Party and Raid Group Sizes

Currently, Drakantos supports a party size of **4 players**. This is the standard group size for most instanced content, including dungeons and initial raid encounters. While the primary focus is on these smaller, coordinated groups, the development team has hinted at the possibility of introducing content that may require larger raid group sizes in the future. This suggests a potential expansion of the endgame experience to accommodate more players in cooperative challenges.

| Content Type | Max Party Size | Future Possibilities |
| :----------- | :------------- | :------------------- |

| Events       | Varies         | Guild vs. Guild events |

### PvP and PvE Hybrid Zones

Drakantos also plans to incorporate diverse PvP experiences, ensuring a competitive environment. Notably, the game aims to balance stats in the Arena to ensure fair play, focusing on player skill rather than gear disparity.

| PvP Mode | Description | Gear Impact |
| :------- | :---------- | :---------- |
| **Arena** | Balanced PvP where player stats are normalized. | **Skill-based**, gear provides no advantage |
| Hellgate  | PvP + PvE zones, offering a mix of combat and exploration. | Gear matters, **risk vs. reward** |

<Callout type="info">
The decision to balance stats in Arena PvP aims to create a highly competitive environment where player skill is the ultimate determinant of victory, a common approach in modern competitive games.

The Drakantos development team is carefully studying monetization options, with a strong commitment to avoiding pay-to-win (P2W) mechanics. Their goal is to ensure that any cash shop items do not provide a competitive advantage in gameplay.
