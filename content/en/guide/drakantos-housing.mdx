<Section icon={Target} title="How to Acquire Your First Plot">

Securing your place in Derid requires a combination of story progression and resource management. Based on the current game structure, players do not start with a home. Instead, you must prove your worth to the local guilds and accumulate enough resources to stake a claim. **Drakantos housing** is gated by "Adventure Rank," ensuring that players have a basic understanding of the game's world before managing a household.

<Steps>

  <Step num="2" title="Gather Essential Resources">

  <Step num="3" title="Locate the Guild Manager">

<Section icon={Users} title="Social Integration and Guild Housing">

Drakantos is fundamentally a multiplayer experience. While personal housing offers a private retreat, the game also features a robust Guild System. Guilds can acquire massive plots of land to build "Guild Halls," which serve as a headquarters for the continent's most powerful factions. Personal **Drakantos housing** can often be linked to these guild halls, creating a neighborhood-like feel for active communities.

**Key Social Features of Housing:**
- **Permission System:** Control who can enter, edit, or interact with your home.
- **House Parties:** Host gatherings where players can showcase their hero's "ending poses" and cosmetics.
- **Trophy Verification:** Friends can inspect your rare items to see exactly which dungeon rank they were earned from.
- **Shared Storage:** Some advanced housing tiers allow for limited item sharing between trusted friends.

<Callout type="info" title="PvP and Housing">
Note that housing is a "Safe Zone." Unlike the competitive ranked PvP arenas, your home cannot be attacked or raided by other players, ensuring your decorations remain permanent.
</Callout>

<Section icon={Sparkles} title="Advanced Tips for Interior Design">

To truly master **Drakantos housing**, you need to look beyond simple placement. The pixel art style allows for clever "layering" techniques. By overlapping certain items or using specific lighting combinations, you can create the illusion of depth in a 2D-inspired space.

**Design Strategies:**
- **Zoning:** Use different flooring types or rugs to separate your "Crafting Area" from your "Lounge Area."
- **Lighting Depth:** Place torches behind semi-transparent objects to create a glowing silhouette effect.
- **Thematic Consistency:** Stick to a color palette that matches your main hero. A Frost mage's home looks best with blue-tinted lighting and stone furniture.
- **Verticality:** Use bookshelves and wall-mounted trophies to draw the eye upward, making small rooms feel larger.
