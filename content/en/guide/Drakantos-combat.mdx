<Section icon={Swords} title="Understanding the New Drakantos Combat System">
The world of Drakantos has undergone significant changes, particularly in its combat mechanics. Players returning from earlier beta phases will find a completely revamped system, moving from a tile-based, hybrid approach to a full action-based combat experience. This update, introduced in the May 2026 Closed Beta, focuses on fluidity, player skill, and dynamic engagements.

This new system emphasizes free movement and direct control, allowing players to attack freely in any direction. Skills now require precise aiming with the mouse, demanding more active participation in every encounter. This shift aims to create a more engaging and responsive combat feel, whether you're navigating PvE challenges or clashing in the new PvP arenas.

<Callout type="tip" title="Key Combat Principle">
Mastering the new **Drakantos combat** system revolves around precise mouse aiming for skills and understanding the stamina-like mechanic for basic attacks. Practice is key to adapting to this more dynamic gameplay.
</Callout>

### Core Combat Changes Overview

Here's a breakdown of the fundamental changes introduced to the Drakantos combat system:

| Feature | Previous System | New Action Combat | Impact on Gameplay |
|---|---|---|---|
| **Movement** | Tile-based | Fully free movement | Enhanced mobility, strategic positioning |
| **Combat Style** | Hybrid (partially action) | Full action-based | More direct control, aim-dependent skills |
| **Targeting** | Target system | No target system | Requires manual aiming for all attacks |
| **Skill Use** | Auto-target/directional | Mouse-aimed | Increased skill ceiling, precision rewarded |
| **Basic Attacks** | Cooldown-based | Stamina-like, use outside cooldown | More continuous damage, timing still optimal |
| **Controller Support** | Limited 8-direction | Smooth 360° movement | Improved fluidity for controller players |

<Section icon={Target} title="Mastering Controls: Keyboard, Mouse, and Controller">
Adapting to the new control scheme is crucial for excelling in **Drakantos combat**. The developers have put considerable effort into refining both keyboard/mouse and controller inputs to provide distinct yet effective gameplay experiences. Understanding the nuances of each will help you choose your preferred method and maximize your effectiveness in battle.

For keyboard and mouse users, the experience is designed to feel intuitive and responsive. Left-click serves as your basic attack, while right-click is dedicated to your special attack, often with an enhanced effect if held down. Your character now automatically faces the direction of your mouse when attacking, streamlining the aiming process.

<Callout type="success" title="Optimizing Your Playstyle">
Experiment with both keyboard/mouse and controller to find what best suits your individual playstyle. While keyboard and mouse might feel easier to pick up initially, the fluid 360° movement offered by a controller can provide a significant advantage in certain situations, especially for dodging and repositioning.
</Callout>

### Control Scheme Breakdown

| Control Aspect | Keyboard & Mouse | Controller | Key Benefits |
|---|---|---|---|
| **Movement** | 8-directional, WASD | Full 360° analog stick | Controller: Superior fluidity, precise dashes |
| **Basic Attack** | Left-click | Face button (e.g., A/X) | Both: Stamina-like, continuous engagement |
| **Special Attack** | Right-click (hold for enhanced) | Trigger/bumper | Both: Strategic use of powerful abilities |
| **Aiming Skills** | Mouse cursor | Right analog stick (turning direction) | Mouse: Direct point-and-click; Controller: Directional aiming |
| **Character Facing** | Auto-faces mouse direction | Controlled by right analog stick | Mouse: Streamlined combat; Controller: Full control over orientation |

<Section icon={Sparkles} title="Advanced Combat Techniques and Tips">
Beyond the basic controls, there are several advanced techniques and considerations that can elevate your **Drakantos combat** performance. Understanding how basic attacks, skills, and even specific hero mechanics interact within the new system is vital for high-level play.

One significant change is how basic attacks function. While they can now be used outside of their cooldown, they operate on a stamina-like system. This means you can continuously attack, but timing your damage around the previous 2-second interval remains the most optimal approach for sustained output. Melee attacks offer a bit more tolerance with charges, allowing for more aggressive engagements.

<Callout type="warning" title="Stamina Management">
Though basic attacks can be used more freely, continuously spamming them without considering the optimal 2-second interval can lead to less effective damage output. Learn to weave in your attacks strategically.
</Callout>

The new movement and combat changes mean many heroes will feel significantly different compared to previous beta iterations. This provides a fresh experience even with familiar content. <Section icon={Trophy} title="Drakantos Combat in PvP: Modes and Mechanics">
The May 2026 Beta introduces players to Drakantos' PvP for the first time, featuring 13 maps across three distinct modes. These modes are designed to test your combat prowess, strategic thinking, and teamwork within the new action system. PvP is also a viable path for progression, allowing players to level up and earn rewards outside of traditional PvE adventures.

In PvP, all players are set to max level with maxed equipment and skills. Trophies and Artifacts are also maximized, though not unlocked in the traditional sense. This ensures a level playing field focused on skill and build choices.

<Callout type="info" title="PvP Equilization">
All participants in PvP are automatically set to max level with maxed gear, skills, trophies, and artifacts. This ensures that matches are determined by player skill and strategic build choices, not by grinding.
</Callout>

### PvP Mode Breakdown

| PvP Mode | Objective | Key Mechanics | Strategic Focus |
|---|---|---|---|
| **Control** | Stand on control zones to earn progress | Progress earned regardless of enemy presence; maps have 1-2 points | Zone control, team coordination, rapid rotation |
| **Escort** | Escort your crystal to the final point | Crystal moves with allies, stops near enemies | Crystal protection, enemy disruption, pathing |
| **Assault** | MOBA-like with towers and a core | Towers share health, prioritize minions; core exposed after towers | Tower destruction, core assault, buff control |

**Additional PvP Considerations:**
*   Assault modes yield more rewards due to longer match durations.
*   Artifact charges are halved in Control and Escort modes.
*   Skills and artifacts with "up to X seconds" effects are typically reduced against players or bosses for balance.
