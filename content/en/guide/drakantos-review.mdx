<Section icon={Target} title="Combat Mechanics: A Critical Drakantos Review">

The combat in Drakantos is currently a point of significant discussion within the community. While the animations are beautiful, the gameplay feels "weighty" in a way that some players find clunky. Specifically, the game utilizes a 4-directional attack system, which can feel restrictive when enemies approach from diagonal angles.

One of the most polarizing features is the **Animation Lock**. Every ability, including the basic auto-attack, prevents your character from moving until the animation completes. For ranged characters like the Ranger, this makes kiting difficult, as you must stop completely to fire a shot.

**Current Combat Feedback & Dev Responses:**

| Issue | Player Sentiment | Developer Status (2026) |
| :--- | :--- | :--- |
| **4-Way Aiming** | Feels restrictive and dated | 8-Way aiming being tested |
| **Animation Lock** | Makes combat feel unresponsive | Looking into animation canceling |
| **Auto-Attack CD** | 2-second cooldown feels slow | Adjusting GCD and attack speed |
| **Body Blocking** | NPCs and players can trap you | Collision logic being reworked |

<Callout type="warning" title="Combat Warning">
Early game combat can feel repetitive until you reach level 12 and unlock a full hotbar of abilities. Persistence is key to seeing the true depth of the hero mechanics.
</Callout>

</Section>

<Section icon={Map} title="Progression and The Adventure System">

Leveling in Drakantos primarily happens through "Adventures"—instanced missions that can be tackled solo or with a group of up to three players. These missions scale in difficulty based on the number of participants and offer rewards including XP, gold, and gear.

<Steps>
  <Step num="1" title="Complete the Tutorial">
    Finish the initial instance with the NPC guide to reach the Adventurers Guild hub.
  </Step>
  <Step num="2" title="Reach Power Level 460">
    Run Rank 1 Adventures to obtain basic gear and increase your total Power Level.
  </Step>
  <Step num="3" title="Unlock Skill Orbs">
    Use Orbs and Artifacts to modify your hero's abilities, adding AOE or CC effects.
  </Step>
  <Step num="4" title="Enter Rank 2 Missions">
    Once geared, move to new zones like the desert to face mini-bosses and tougher mobs.
  </Step>
</Steps>

The "Power Level" system is the primary metric for character strength. Currently, equipment lacks deep secondary stats (like crit rate or haste), focusing instead on a singular power score. However, the community has strongly suggested the addition of gem slots and enchanting to allow for more character customization.

<Callout type="info" title="Solo vs Group">
Adventures automatically group you with other players by default. While this speeds up clear times, it also scales enemy health significantly. Some players prefer soloing to maintain control over the pace.
</Callout>

</Section>
