<Section icon={AlertTriangle} title="Understanding Drakantos Input Delay">
Input delay, often a source of frustration for gamers, can significantly impact your performance and enjoyment in fast-paced titles like Drakantos. This issue can manifest as a noticeable lag between your actions (e.g., clicking a mouse or pressing a key) and the corresponding response in the game. In Drakantos, where precise movement and combat are crucial, even a slight delay can lead to missed attacks, failed dodges, or overall sluggish gameplay.

During its beta phases, Drakantos underwent significant gameplay changes, moving from tile-based movement to free movement and revamping controls for full action combat. While these changes aimed for a smoother and more fluid experience, some players reported feeling "laggy/delayed movement that isn't snappy" and "awkward and slow combat." This feedback highlights the importance of optimizing your setup to minimize input delay, especially for abilities like dashes that require quick, responsive execution.

<Callout type="warning" title="Beta Phase Specifics">
During early beta tests, the Drakantos development team acknowledged that major gameplay changes led to significantly heavier server loads. They implemented a queue system to manage stability while scaling server capacity. This often means that early access or beta periods might inherently have more latency issues than the full release.
</Callout>

<Section icon={Target} title="Step-by-Step Troubleshooting for Drakantos Input Delay">
Follow these steps to systematically diagnose and resolve input delay issues you might be experiencing in Drakantos.

<Steps>
  <Step num="1" title="Optimize Network Connection">
    A stable and fast internet connection is paramount for online gaming.
    - **Use a wired connection:** Ethernet cables provide a more stable and faster connection than Wi-Fi, reducing latency and packet loss.
    - **Check your ping:** Use in-game or external tools (like `ping google.com` in Command Prompt) to monitor your latency. High ping (above 50-80ms) will always result in noticeable delay.
    - **Close bandwidth-heavy applications:** Streaming services, large downloads, or other online games can consume bandwidth, impacting your game's performance.
    - **Restart your router/modem:** A simple restart can often resolve temporary network glitches.
  </Step>
  <Step num="2" title="Update Drivers and System Software">
    Outdated drivers can lead to performance bottlenecks and input issues.
    - **Graphics Card Drivers:** Update to the latest drivers from NVIDIA, AMD, or Intel. These updates often include game-specific optimizations.
    - **Operating System:** Ensure your Windows (or other OS) is fully updated.
    - **Peripheral Drivers:** Check for updates for your mouse, keyboard, or controller.
  </Step>
  <Step num="3" title="Adjust In-Game Settings">
    Drakantos offers various settings that can impact performance.
    - **Graphics Settings:** Lowering settings like resolution, texture quality, shadow quality, and anti-aliasing can significantly boost FPS and reduce perceived input delay.
    - **V-Sync:** While V-Sync prevents screen tearing, it often introduces input lag. Try disabling it and see if the responsiveness improves. If tearing is an issue, consider using G-Sync or FreeSync if your monitor supports it.
    - **Mouse/Controller Sensitivity:** Experiment with these settings to find a comfortable and responsive level.
  </Step>
  <Step num="4" title="Check Hardware Performance">
    Ensure your PC can handle Drakantos smoothly.
    - **Monitor CPU/GPU Usage:** Use tools like Task Manager (Windows) or MSI Afterburner to see if your CPU or GPU are consistently at 100% usage during gameplay. High usage indicates a bottleneck.
    - **RAM:** Ensure you have sufficient RAM. Drakantos, as an MMORPG, might benefit from 16GB RAM or more.
    - **Storage:** While less impactful on input delay directly, a slow HDD can cause longer loading times and occasional stutters. An SSD is always recommended for gaming.
  </Step>
  <Step num="5" title="Minimize Background Processes">
    Unnecessary applications running in the background can compete for resources.
    - **Task Manager:** Open Task Manager (Ctrl+Shift+Esc) and close any non-essential applications or processes that are consuming significant CPU, RAM, or disk resources.
    - **Startup Programs:** Disable unnecessary programs from launching with Windows via Task Manager's "Startup" tab.
    - **Game Mode:** Enable Windows Game Mode (Settings > Gaming > Game Mode) to prioritize game performance.
  </Step>
</Steps>
