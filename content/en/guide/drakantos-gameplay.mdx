### Character Roles and Progression

Drakantos features a variety of heroes, each with different roles such as DPS, tanks, and supports, some of whom offer healing capabilities. Players can freely switch between heroes, though each new hero starts at level one with no gear, encouraging exploration of different playstyles.

Progression in Drakantos involves:
- **Leveling up heroes**: Gaining experience through quests and adventures.
- **Acquiring artifacts**: These provide unique side skills or buffs and are often consumable but replenish after missions.
- **Upgrading abilities**: Spending skill points to enhance hero capabilities.
- **Customizing skills**: Using orbs to modify abilities, adding variations like faster cast speeds or altered attack directions.

<Callout type="info" title="Ability Unlock Changes">
During playtests, the developer adjusted ability unlock levels from 5, 10, 15 to 4, 8, 12, making early game progression feel more rewarding.
</Callout>

<Section icon={Swords} title="Combat and Movement in Drakantos">
The combat system in Drakantos has been a significant point of discussion during playtests. While the high-level combat with a full hotbar of abilities feels more engaging, the early game can feel slow and clunky. A notable aspect is the animation lock on almost every ability, including basic attacks, which can hinder fluid movement and kiting, especially for ranged DPS characters.

### Core Combat Mechanics

| Mechanic          | Description                                                                 | Impact on Gameplay                                     |
| :---------------- | :-------------------------------------------------------------------------- | :----------------------------------------------------- |
| **Basic Attack Cooldown** | Most heroes have a 2-second cooldown on basic attacks.                      | Limits continuous damage, makes early combat feel slow. |
| **Animation Lock** | Nearly all abilities, including basic attacks, lock character movement.      | Reduces fluidity, difficult to kite enemies effectively. |
| **Directional Attacks** | Attacks are primarily in four cardinal directions (some shoot in a straight line). | Can feel restrictive, especially against diagonal approaches. |
| **Artifacts**     | Consumable items that grant temporary skills or summons (e.g., summoning orcs). | Adds tactical depth, can significantly impact combat flow. |
| **Skill Orbs**    | Modifiers that change an ability's properties (e.g., faster cast speed, AoE). | Customizes playstyle, improves late-game combat feel.   |

<Callout type="warning" title="Early Game Combat Feedback">
Many players found the early game combat to be basic and boring due to limited abilities and animation locks. The developers have acknowledged feedback regarding clunkiness and movement responsiveness, with fixes planned for future tests and full release.
</Callout>

### Movement and Interactions

Movement in Drakantos has also received feedback. Players noted a delayed and unresponsive feeling, sometimes akin to a "tick system" rather than smooth, continuous motion. Another issue observed was body blocking by both NPCs and party members, which further contributes to the clunky movement experience.

<Section icon={Trophy} title="PvE Adventures and Dungeon System">
Drakantos features a combination of open-world exploration and instanced multiplayer adventures. These adventures are typically 3-man parties where players work together to complete objectives, earn XP, loot, and resources.

### Adventure Structure

Adventures are dynamic, with missions changing over time to offer varying sets of enemies, bosses, resources, traps, and objectives. This ensures that every playthrough feels unique and challenging. However, some playtest feedback indicated that many adventures felt like mundane tasks and lacked boss fights, leading to a desire for more engaging content.

### Loot and Gear Progression

Gear progression in Drakantos primarily relies on "stat sticks"—equipping items like hats to increase power level without altering character appearance. This system is straightforward but might lack the visual customization many MMORPG players expect. Artifacts also contribute significantly to power and tactical options.

<Section icon={Shield} title="PvP System and Competitive Play">
For players seeking to test their skills against others, Drakantos offers a robust PvP system with various game modes, a ranked system, and regular in-game tournaments. The PvP environment is designed to be skill-based, with stats balanced to ensure a competitive playing field.

### PvP Modes and Mechanics

Drakantos PvP includes modes like "Assault" and "Control." In PvP, hero choice is crucial, but artifact loadouts play a significant role in gaining an advantage. Artifacts that summon monsters or provide continuous healing (like a campfire) can heavily influence match outcomes.
