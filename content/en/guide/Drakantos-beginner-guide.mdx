<Section icon={Swords} title="Choosing Your First Hero: Roles and Abilities">
In Drakantos, you don't create a custom character. Instead, you select from a roster of unique heroes, each with their own name, story, and distinct abilities. These heroes fall into typical MMORPG archetypes: DPS, Tank, and Support. Understanding these roles is key to selecting a hero that matches your preferred playstyle.

<Callout type="tip">
Experiment with a few different heroes early on. Since progress is character-specific, you can always switch and try another if your initial choice doesn't feel right.
</Callout>

The game typically unlocks a hero's full potential, including their ultimate ability, around level 12. This is when their unique class identity truly shines.

**Hero Archetypes and Examples:**

| Archetype | Description | Example Hero Traits | Playstyle |
| :-------- | :------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------- | :-------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------- | :---------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------- |
| **DPS**   | Focuses on dealing high damage to enemies. Often has abilities for crowd control or movement. | Ranged attacker with fire-setting crossbow, jetpack for kiting, landmines, turrets. (e.g., Orberin) | Aggressive, positioning-focused, relies on quick attacks and skill rotations. |
| **Tank**  | Specializes in absorbing damage and protecting allies. Can taunt enemies and reduce incoming damage. | Sword and shield for blocking, taunt abilities, high health pool. (e.g., Zeugladius) | Frontline, defensive, crowd control, requires strong threat management. |
| **Support** | Provides healing, buffs, and debuffs to aid the team. Essential for sustained combat. | Area-of-effect heals, holy spells, utility skills. (e.g., Reya) | Backline, reactive, focuses on ally protection and strategic spellcasting. |

Each hero has a unique set of skills that unlock as they level up. For instance, a ranged DPS might get a jetpack for mobility, while a tank could gain powerful defensive stances.

<Section icon={Target} title="Progression and Core Gameplay Loop">
The core progression in Drakantos revolves around running 3-man dungeons, gathering resources in the open world, and upgrading your gear. Unlike many MMORPGs, experience gain is primarily from dungeons, not open-world mob grinding.

<Callout type="warning">
While the open world is vast, grinding mobs there won't grant significant experience. Its main purpose is resource gathering for gear upgrades and completing quests.
</Callout>

### Dungeon Running
Dungeons are instanced 3-man missions that provide the bulk of your experience and loot. They refresh every 20-30 minutes, offering a variety of mission types.

**Types of Dungeon Missions:**

| Mission Type      | Objective                                                                        | Rewards                                                                                              |
| :---------------- | :------------------------------------------------------------------------------- | :--------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------- |
| **Exterminate**   | Clear all mobs within the dungeon.                                               | High XP, varied gear drops, crafting materials.                                                      |
| **Escort**        | Protect a crystal or NPC to a designated point.                                  | Moderate XP, specific gear pieces, unique artifacts.                                                 |
| **Item Hunt**     | Locate and collect scattered items throughout the map.                           | Lower XP, rare crafting ingredients, potential for experience boosters.                              |

To maximize your dungeon rewards, aim to clear all mobs. Dying in a mission incurs an experience loss (around 5% per death), so play carefully.

### Open World Activities
The open world in Drakantos serves as a hub for quests and resource gathering. You'll find mining nodes, trees for wood, and chests to lockpick, all providing materials for gear reinforcement.

<Callout type="tip">
Regularly explore the open world for hidden chests. These can contain valuable items like experience boosters or rare crafting components, which can significantly speed up your progression.
</Callout>

</Section>

<Section icon={Gem} title="Gear, Artifacts, and Orbs: Powering Up Your Hero">
Your hero's power largely depends on their gear score, artifacts, and orbs. These systems are crucial for making your character stronger and customizing your playstyle.

### Gear System
Gear in Drakantos is straightforward, consisting of a hat, top, shoes, and a weapon. Gear score is paramount; a higher gear score means better performance, regardless of your character's actual level.

**Gear Progression Steps:**

<Steps>
  <Step num="1" title="Obtain Base Gear">
    Acquire initial gear pieces from early dungeons or quests. Focus on getting a full set of basic equipment.
  </Step>
  <Step num="2" title="Gather Materials">
    Venture into the open world to collect materials like wood, ore, and other resources from mining nodes, trees, and chests.
  </Step>
  <Step num="3" title="Reinforce Gear">
    Visit the blacksmith or designated NPC to use your gathered materials and reinforce your gear. This increases its gear score and stats.
  </Step>
  <Step num="4" title="Progress to Higher Tiers">
    As your gear score increases, you'll gain access to more difficult dungeons that drop higher-tier equipment, repeating the cycle.
  </Step>
</Steps>

<Callout type="success">
Always reinforce your gear whenever possible. Gear score directly impacts your effectiveness in dungeons, making content easier and more rewarding.
</Callout>

### Artifacts and Orbs
Artifacts are special items that grant unique abilities, allowing for significant playstyle customization. You can equip two artifacts at a time. Orbs are another form of progression that slot into specific skills, boosting their effects.

**Artifact Examples:**

| Artifact Name | Effect                                                               | Playstyle Impact                                                                                                 |
| :------------ | :------------------------------------------------------------------- | :--------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------- |
| **Beer Keg**  | Makes enemies attack each other.                                     | Excellent for crowd control and reducing incoming damage, especially in large pulls.                             |
| **Wasp Nest** | Deals damage over time to enemies.                                   | Good for consistent damage output, particularly against bosses or tanky foes.                                    |
| **Chains**    | Chains enemies to your character, limiting their movement.           | Useful for keeping enemies grouped for AoE attacks or preventing them from reaching squishy allies.             |
| **Healing Flask** | Provides a burst of healing over time.                           | Essential for survivability, especially during tough encounters or when playing solo.                             |
| **Stun Crossbow** | Shoots an enemy, stunning them.                                  | Great for interrupting dangerous enemy abilities or creating openings for burst damage.                          |

Orbs are found during dungeon runs and are character-bound once used. They provide direct enhancements to specific skills, such as allowing you to hold down an attack for more damage or increasing the potency of a defensive ability.

<Section icon={Shield} title="Combat and Movement: Adapting to Drakantos' Style">
Drakantos features a distinct combat and movement system that new players will need to adapt to. The game transitioned from tile-based movement to fully free movement, and controls are now action-based, allowing for aiming skills with the mouse.

### Combat Mechanics
Combat is action-oriented, requiring you to aim your skills. Left-click is for basic attacks, and right-click for special attacks (which can often be held for enhanced effects).

<Callout type="info">
While the game features action combat, the movement can feel stiff or "grid-based" initially. This is intentional and adds a tactical layer, requiring precise positioning and timing of abilities.
</Callout>

**Key Combat Tips:**
- **Keyboard-driven:** Combat is primarily controlled via keyboard, with mouse aiming. Get used to this setup for optimal performance.
- **Skill timing:** Pay attention to cooldowns and the tactical nature of combat. It's not a button-mashing game.
- **Dodging:** Many classes have dash skills that can be used to avoid enemy attacks, which is crucial for survival.
- **Party synergy:** In dungeons, coordinate with your team (Tank, DPS, Support) to maximize efficiency and minimize deaths.

### Movement
Movement in Drakantos can initially feel a bit clunky due to its grid-based roots, but the developers have refined it for a more fluid experience, especially with controller support.

**Movement Options:**

| Control Type      | Movement Style                                                                  | Advantages                                                                                                | Disadvantages                                                                     |
| :---------------- | :------------------------------------------------------------------------------ | :-------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------- | :-------------------------------------------------------------------------------- |
| **Keyboard**      | Limited to 8 directions initially, but now offers more freedom with action combat. | Familiar for PC gamers, precise for aiming skills.                                                        | Can feel less fluid than controller for movement.                                 |
| **Controller**    | Full 360° movement, smoother and more fluid.                                    | Ideal for agile characters, allows for better positioning and dodging.                                     | May take time to adapt if you're a traditional keyboard/mouse player.             |

<Callout type="warning">
If you're struggling with movement, consider trying a controller. Many players find it offers a more fluid and responsive experience, especially for characters with dashes.
</Callout>
