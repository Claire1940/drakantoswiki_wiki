<Section icon={Swords} title="Introduction to Drakantos PvP Mechanics">

The competitive landscape of **drakantos pvp** is designed to prioritize player skill over raw grinding. Unlike the PvE dungeons where gear power levels dictate success, the arena environment equalizes base stats. This means a level 15 hero and a level 50 hero enter the match on relatively even footing regarding health and damage, shifting the focus to ability timing, positioning, and team synergy. This approach, inspired by titles like Overwatch and League of Legends, ensures that every match is a test of strategy rather than time investment.

### The Core Pillars of Combat

Success in the arena requires more than just button mashing. Because the game has moved away from a strict grid-based movement system, manual aiming and "body blocking" have become essential tactics. Tanks can physically stand in the way of projectiles or enemy movement to protect squishier teammates, adding a layer of depth to the 2D pixel-art combat.

| Feature | PvP Implementation | Impact on Gameplay |
| :--- | :--- | :--- |
| **Stats** | Equalized | Skill and strategy outweigh gear level |
| **Movement** | Free-form (Non-grid) | Better dodging and manual aiming |
| **Body Blocking** | Enabled | Tanks can physically shield allies |
| **Abilities** | 4-5 Active Skills | Requires precise cooldown management |
| **Artifacts** | Active Consumables | Provides unique utility like summons |

<Section icon={Shield} title="Hero Roles and the Level 12 Threshold">

With 21 different heroes available, team composition is the cornerstone of **drakantos pvp**. However, players often find the early game "vanilla" because many heroes do not unlock their most impactful utility skills until Level 12. For example, a Paladin is primarily a basic attacker until they unlock their dedicated healing or taunt abilities.

<Steps>
  <Step num="1" title="Initial Leveling">
    Focus on PvE dungeons to reach Level 12 quickly. This ensures your hero has their full kit available for competitive play.
  </Step>
  <Step num="2" title="Skill Customization">
    Use Orbs to modify your skills. For example, some Orbs can change a spinning attack's direction or increase cast speed.
  </Step>
  <Step num="3" title="Role Identification">
    Determine if your hero is a Main Tank, Off-Tank, or Pure DPS. This dictates your positioning in the arena.
  </Step>
  <Step num="4" title="Synergy Testing">
    Queue with friends to test how your Level 12 skills interact, such as a Tank's taunt pulling enemies into a Mage's AoE.
  </Step>
</Steps>

<Callout type="warning" title="Progression Warning">
Switching heroes is free, but new heroes start at Level 1. You will lose access to high-level PvP utility until you grind that specific hero back to Level 12.
</Callout>

<Section icon={Sparkles} title="The Power of Artifacts and Orbs">

Since base stats are equalized in **drakantos pvp**, your "edge" comes from Artifacts and Orbs. These systems allow for deep build customization, turning a standard hero into a specialized threat. Artifacts act as active items with long cooldowns that replenish after every match or mission.

<CardGrid cols={3}>
  <Card title="Summoner Artifacts">
    Spawns bears, orcs, or snakes. These are massive in PvP for distracting towers or overwhelming opponents in a 3v1.
  </Card>
  <Card title="Sustain Artifacts">
    The Campfire is the gold standard, providing a localized heal-over-time that can turn the tide during a capture point contest.
  </Card>
  <Card title="Utility Artifacts">
    Items like the Beehive provide area denial, making it difficult for enemies to stand on objectives without taking chip damage.
  </Card>
</CardGrid>

</Callout>
