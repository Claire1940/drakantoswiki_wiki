The roster is balanced across three primary roles. While the initial closed beta tests featured a limited selection, the full release aims to expand this list significantly. <Section icon={Palette} title="Visual Customization and Dyes">

While you cannot change the facial structure or height of your hero, **Drakantos character creation** offers visual agency through its cosmetic systems. The developers have implemented a layer of personalization that allows players to stand out in the game's 2D pixel-art world.

The primary method of customization is the **Dye System**. This allows you to modify specific hex-color zones on your hero's sprite.

| Customization Type | Description | How to Obtain |
| :--- | :--- | :--- |
| **Armor Dyes** | Changes the primary and secondary colors of your gear | Crafting, drops, or Adventurer's Guild |
| **Hair Dyes** | Modifies the base hair color of the hero | Vanity shop or special event rewards |
| **Costumes** | Full-body skins that override gear appearance | In-game store or rare dungeon drops |
| **Vanity Items** | Small accessories like hats or back pieces | Achievement rewards and guild tokens |

<Section icon={Sparkles} title="Skill Customization via Orbs and Artifacts">

True "creation" in Drakantos happens after the selection screen. The game utilizes a modular skill system that allows you to modify how your hero's abilities function. This is handled through two primary items: **Orbs** and **Artifacts**.

At Level 12, your hero unlocks their defining class skill (such as a taunt for tanks or a major heal for supports). From this point, you begin finding Orbs that can be slotted directly into your active skills.

| Item Type | Slotting Method | Effect on Gameplay |
| :--- | :--- | :--- |
| **Skill Orbs** | Sockets into specific abilities | Increases damage, reduces cooldowns, or adds effects |
| **Artifacts** | Two dedicated equipment slots | Grants new active/passive skills (e.g., Wasp Nest DOT) |
| **Reinforcements** | Applied at the blacksmith | Boosts the base power and Gear Score of equipment |

<Callout type="success" title="The Level 12 Power Spike">
Reaching Level 12 is the most critical milestone in your character's early life. This is when the "stiff" feeling of early combat dissipates as you gain the full rotation of skills necessary for tactical dungeon clearing.
</Callout>

Artifacts act as "sub-skills" that refresh every time you exit a dungeon. For example, a Tank might equip an artifact that causes enemies to attack each other, providing additional crowd control that isn't part of their base kit. This allows for hybrid builds, such as a "Brawler Support" or a "Tanky DPS."

</Section>

<Section icon={Shield} title="Gear Score and Equipment Progression">

In Drakantos, your character's power is represented by a **Gear Score**. This number is more important than your actual character level when determining your effectiveness in high-tier dungeons. The equipment system is streamlined into four main slots plus two artifact slots.

| Slot | Impact | Primary Source |
| :--- | :--- | :--- |
| **Weapon** | Damage output and skill scaling | Boss drops and crafting |
| **Hat** | Health and mana pools | Dungeon chests |
| **Top** | Physical and magical defense | Quest rewards and drops |
| **Shoes** | Movement speed and dodge cooldown | Open world gathering/crafting |

<Steps>
  <Step num="1" title="Grind Tier 1 Dungeons">
    Run 3-man instances to collect a full set of basic gear. Focus on clearing all mobs to maximize loot drops.
  </Step>
  <Step num="2" title="Gather Resources">
    Head to the open world to mine ores and chop trees. These materials are required to reinforce your gear at the blacksmith.
  </Step>
  <Step num="3" title="Boost Gear Score">
    Use the gathered materials to upgrade your Tier 1 gear. This unlocks access to Tier 2 dungeons with better experience rates.
  </Step>
  <Step num="4" title="Socket Orbs">
    As you find Skill Orbs, slot them immediately. Unlike gear, Orbs provide permanent boosts to your hero's abilities.
  </Step>
</Steps>

<Callout type="info" title="Tactical Movement">
Drakantos uses a grid-based movement system that can feel "stiff" or "tactical" depending on your preference. It is highly recommended to play with a **controller** to better manage the timing of your dashes and skill execution.
</Callout>
